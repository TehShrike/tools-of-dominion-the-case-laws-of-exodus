Burns, a political scientist at the University of Toronto, has summa-
rized the problem: "Between 1966 and 1971 the U. S. murder rate in-
creased by 52%, and the crime rate as a whole rose by 74%, as re-
ported in Crime in the United States: Uniform Crime Reports) 1971.
Crimes of violence (murder, forcible rape, robbery and aggravated
assault) went up 80%. In 1971 there were 5,995,200 index crimes
(crimes catalogued by the FBI) reported to the police, and everyone
knows that a large number of crimes are never reported to the police.
The proportion of arrests to crimes reported was only 19 %, persons
charged 17%, persons convicted as charged 5%, and persons con-
victed of lesser offenses .9%. All of which means that punishment
was meted out in only 5.7% of the known cases of crime. The conclu-
sion is inescapable: crime pays. Moreover, some authorities insist that
